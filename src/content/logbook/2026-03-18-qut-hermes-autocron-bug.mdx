Hermes `auto_cron` entries in `config.yaml` are declarations-only — they don't actually execute. Two cron jobs silently never ran for 6 days after migration.

<br />

### What happened

After migrating from launchd plists to Hermes cron, `daily_reflection` (09:00 daily) and `weekly_habit_observer` (10:00 Sundays) were declared in the `auto_cron` section of `~/.hermes/config.yaml`. They looked correct. No errors on startup. But they never ran.

Discovery came during a post-migration verification audit — checking which jobs had actually produced output. Both jobs had zero runs.

---

<br />

### Root cause

`auto_cron` in `config.yaml` is a **declaration** — it tells Hermes what jobs *could* exist. But only entries in `jobs.json` (the runtime job store) actually get scheduled and executed. Declaring a job in config.yaml does not create it in jobs.json.

The other 9 cron jobs worked because they had been explicitly created via `hermes cron create` during the migration. These two were added to config.yaml afterwards, assuming the config declaration was sufficient.

---

<br />

### Fix

```bash
hermes cron create --name "daily-reflection" \
  --schedule "0 9 * * *" \
  --prompt "Run daily reflection"

hermes cron create --name "weekly-habit-observer" \
  --schedule "0 10 * * 0" \
  --prompt "Run weekly habit observer"
```

Verified with `hermes cron list` — both now appear and execute on schedule.

---

<br />

### Lesson

Declarative config does not equal imperative execution. Always verify with `hermes cron list` after adding jobs — if it's not in the list output, it's not running. The `auto_cron` section is useful as documentation but misleading as a source of truth.
